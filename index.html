RGB is a way of displaying colors on a computer screen using combinations of red, green, and blue. Each base color can have a value from 0 to 255, meaning there are 16,777,216 or 256^3 possible RGB colors.

Here, you get 864,000 of them (10 per second) over the course of a day: blue cycles every minute, green every hour, and red every 24 hours. Check back at different times and see what else you notice!
